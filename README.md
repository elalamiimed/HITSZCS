### Overview

The **HITSZCS** repository is a comprehensive resource hub for Harbin Institute of Technology (Shenzhen Campus) students. This repository contains:

- **Course Materials**: Lecture slides, lecture notes, and learning resources for all major courses
- **Homework & Assignments**: Homework problems, solutions, and assignment guidelines
- **Exam Preparation**: Practice exams, model tests, and study guides
- **Books & References**: Links to textbooks and recommended reading materials
- **Lab Resources**: Lab instructions and tutorial materials for practical courses
- **Multilingual Support**: Resources for both regular students and international students
- **Community Contributions**: Student-created study materials and helpful resources

This is a **community-driven repository** where students actively contribute and share resources to help each other succeed. Whether you're looking for course materials, exam preparation resources, or just need clarification on difficult topics, you'll likely find it here.

Please follow the instructions below to navigate GitHub and find key files in the repository.

### Repository Structure

The repository is organized by **academic year and course type** to help you find materials relevant to your stage of study:

**By Academic Year:**
- **`Freshman/`**: First-year courses including Calculus, Linear Algebra, College Physics IA, Electric Circuits, Pre-Calculus, and introductory language courses
- **`Sophomore/`**: Second-year courses including College Physics IB, Data Structures, Computer Networks, Probability & Statistics, and advanced language courses
- **`Junior/`**: Third-year courses and advanced specializations
- **`Elective Courses/`**: Optional courses available to all students

**Curriculum Paths:**
- **`Curriculum/`**: General curriculum information and guidelines
- **`For International Students/`**: Special resources and support for exchange and international students
- **`JoinUs/`**: Information for new students and joining the community

**Available Resources in Each Course Folder:**
- `Slides/` or `Lessons/` - Lecture materials and notes
- `Homework/` - Assignment problems and deadlines
- `Book/` - Textbook references and PDFs
- `Quiz/` - Quiz preparation materials
- `Resources/` - Additional study materials
- `Model Test/` or `Exam prep/` - Practice exams and exam reviews
- `Tutorials/` - Lab and practical tutorials
- `README.md` - Course-specific information and syllabus

### Navigating GitHub

1. **Viewing Files**: 
   - On the main **HITSZCS** page, click on any folder or file name to view its contents. Files with a `.md` extension, like `README.md` or `AboutUs.md`, are Markdown files and will display directly on GitHub for easy reading.

2. **Using the File Structure**:
   - The repository is organized with folders for different academic years and courses. For example, the `Freshman/` folder contains resources specifically for first-year students. To view these, simply click on the folder.
   - Within each course folder, you'll find organized subfolders for lectures, homework, exams, and more.

3. **Finding Your Course**:
   - Identify your academic year (Freshman, Sophomore, Junior)
   - Find your specific course within that year's folder
   - Look for the relevant subfolder (Slides, Homework, etc.)

4. **Returning to the Main Page**:
   - To go back to the main **HITSZCS** page at any time, click the repository name at the top.

### How to Use This Repository

**As a Student Looking for Materials:**
1. Navigate to your academic year folder (Freshman, Sophomore, Junior)
2. Find your specific course
3. Look for the relevant subfolder (Slides, Homework, Exam prep, etc.)
4. Download or view the files you need
5. Check the course `README.md` for additional information and guidelines

**Finding Specific Content:**
- **For Exams**: Look in the `Exam prep/`, `Model Test/`, or `Finals Materials/` folders
- **For Practice**: Check the `Homework/` folders for practice problems
- **For Learning**: Browse `Slides/`, `Lessons/`, or `Resources/` folders
- **For References**: Look in `Book/` folders for textbook information and links

**Need Help?**
- Check the relevant course `README.md` first - most courses have specific guidance
- Review `AboutUs.md` for repository information
- See `Contribution.md` if you want to contribute materials
- Check `Library.md` for a comprehensive index of all available materials
